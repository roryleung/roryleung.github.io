/ˈɹɔːɹi syː˨ sɐm˥ lœːŋ˩ /

I research how people learn second languages, and outside of research, I still teach.

I grew up in Hong Kong, where East meets West and languages naturally flow into one another. That landscape shaped the way I see the world. Language has never felt like something fixed on a textbook page to me. It feels more fluid than that: a current between people, carrying thoughts, knowledge, feelings and small pieces of who we are.

Language is the current between minds.

What I'm working on

sidestreams

Freestyle and coxing

Freestyle

Outside of research, I dance and battle as a street dancer, mostly in waacking. In 2023, I won the Break Mission 1-on-1 battle in Birmingham and placed second at A Soulful Night Vol. 2 in Vienna. I relish the feeling of turning emotion into movement, and of finding meaning through rhythm, response and style. Dance, for me, is another language: it has its own vocabulary, grammar and turn-taking. In freestyle, movement flows like conversation, carrying what words sometimes cannot.

Coxing

I also cox occasionally on the Cam. Coxing has taught me how quickly words can slip away under pressure, and how much it matters to find, monitor and adjust them in the moment. It is where Levelt’s (1989) model of speech production feels less like theory and more like survival. My highlight was the 2024–25 season, when we bladed at May Bumps after a term of stress, pressure and hard work, finding our rhythm together on the river.
